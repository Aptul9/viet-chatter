## Extracted facts

`extracted_facts` is an array. Emit only what genuinely improves future turns. Prefer zero facts over noise.

Each fact: `{ tier, content, confidence, ttl_days?, supersedes_id?, anchor_date?, anchor_recurring?, anchor_action? }`.

`content` is a single English sentence, 1-2 lines max, neutral and factual. Storage language is English regardless of `language_used`.

### Tier rules

- `important` â€” rare, life-defining changes about the person or their close circle: job change, move, breakup, marriage, death, birth, serious illness, major achievement, a stated birthday or anniversary. Confidence must be `>= 0.85`. If unsure, downgrade to `secondary` or skip.
- `secondary` â€” recurring details that may matter again: hobbies, work projects, food preferences, recurring people in their life, recent travel, sport teams, taste. Confidence `>= 0.7`.
- `ephemeral` â€” short-lived states: plans this week, current mood, "just got back from X", "tomorrow has a meeting". Always set `ttl_days` (positive integer). Default `7`. Max `30`.

### Anti-duplicate

- Before emitting, scan `kb.important`, `kb.ephemeral`, and `kb.secondary` for a fact that already states the same thing. If present and still accurate, DO NOT re-extract.
- If the existing fact needs updating (value changed, status changed, more precise version), emit ONE new fact with `supersedes_id` set to the existing fact's id. The fact id is the numeric prefix shown in KB entries when available; if not visible, omit `supersedes_id` and just emit the corrected fact at the appropriate tier â€” the bot will dedupe.
- Do not emit ephemeral facts that simply restate something already in active `kb.ephemeral`.

### Anchors (for `date_anchored` jobs)

- `anchor_date` formats:
  - `YYYY-MM-DD` for one-shot future events (e.g. `"2026-09-14"` for a specific upcoming meeting or trip).
  - `MM-DD` for recurring annual events (birthdays, anniversaries).
- When `anchor_date` is in `MM-DD` form, set `anchor_recurring: "yearly"`. Otherwise set it to `null` or omit.
- Always set `anchor_action` to a short snake_case verb describing what the user should do at fire time. Examples: `"wish_birthday"`, `"wish_anniversary"`, `"follow_up"`, `"check_in_after_trip"`.
- Only set anchors on `important` facts unless the date is the whole point of a `secondary` fact (rare).

### Quality bar

- Emit a fact only if you would bet money it is correct and useful.
- Skip vague impressions, conversational noise, jokes, hypotheticals, hearsay about people other than the person you are writing to (unless directly relevant, e.g. a close family member).
- Empty array `[]` is a valid and frequent output.
